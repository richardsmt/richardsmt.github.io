Why so many coins ?

August 14, 2017

At the time of writing, there are 1059 cryptocurrencies listed of the coinmarketcap.com, ( the go-to website for price, market information ). The list starts with Bitcoin, Ethereum and Ripple at the top and ends with ELcoin, microCoin and Turbocoin.

The question : why do many coins ? Do we really need so many different digital currencies ? Is it even conceivable that even 50 of them have a stable long term future.

The question is especially important not only for traders and especially individual investors who are switching for traditional savings accounts and investment tools like stocks to the new crypto arena.

Having dozens, not hundreds of new blockchain projects might not be a problem. Indeed it is a sign of a booming sector poised to take center stage. Many existing web and non-web technologies can and will be disrupted by these projets.

However I still don’t see the case for 1059 crypto-currencies. It’s one thing to create a new protocol, a new app or a new service. It’s an entirely different proposition to maintain yet another means of payment and store of value.

Let’s take file storage projects like Sia, Storj or Filecoin. Decentralized and distributed file storage is undoubtedly a great idea that caters to an increasing pressure on traditional cloud providers. Privacy and also resilience by reducing the number of points of failure are enough arguments to support the emergence of these projets.

But the question remains : why do they need their own currency? In other words what would Sia, Storj and Filecoin loose by accepting Bitcoin or Ethereum as payments for their services ?

Even if one could find an internal justification, the existence of hundreds of currencies could turn into a nightmare for individuals and organizations about to embrace the wave of new blockchain services.

Let’s picture a scenario : a small-to-medium business, led by a visionary CEO, chooses to fully embrace the blockchain. They use it for file storage, identity management, contract resolution, energy management and even for ordering pizza (please tell there is such a thing as PizzaCoin). In the end they end up using a dozens such servies each with their own coin.

The end result would be an accounting and even a purchasing nightmare. That organisation would have to maintain a dozen crypto-currency accounts. But the the biggest issue would be cost scheduling. Crypto-cuurnecy markets are volatile. Individual coins go up and down in value on an hourly basis. Your file storage service could end up costing twice as much relative to fiat (or bitcoin for that matter) from one month to the next. Right now large price movements happen one week to the next if not daily.

The alternative to this scenario sees that same company using the same services but paying for them with Bitcoin or Ethereum.

Independently of the value proposition of the myriad of new blockchain technologies we need to ask of these new projects : why do we need another coin ?
